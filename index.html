About

I am a postdoctoral fellow at the California Institute of Technology. My work sits at the interface of quantum information and condensed matter theory, focusing on long‑range entanglement and constraints from symmetry and locality. Recent interests include quantum anomalies and crystalline responses in topological (semi)metals, finite‑depth circuit obstructions (e.g., non‑zero momentum ⇒ LRE), permutation‑invariant codes/W‑states, and exactly constrained lattice fermions.

Research

  • Symmetry, circuits, and phases: Constraints from locality and symmetry on finite‑depth circuit preparation; momentum‑carrying states and LRE; LSM‑type consequences.
  • Topological semimetals: Quantum anomalies, crystalline responses, and interaction‑enabled phases (Dirac/Weyl systems).
  • Codes & invariant subspaces: Permutation‑invariant codes (Dicke/W), robustness, and connections to circuit lower bounds.
